# Completed Test Ideal Decision Gate

## Outcome

The finite-place and score-projection stages now have concrete Lean
inhabitants. The exact completed projection does not yet. The obstruction is
no longer an unspecified "test topology": it is the choice of a common
Fourier domain on which the finite score, Gamma multiplier, pole moments, and
Weil self-convolutions are all simultaneously defined.

## Finite-Place Domain

`CompletedLogTest` is Mathlib's bundled `C_c(R, R)`. Every such test has a
proved support radius and canonical prime and power cutoffs. Consequently,

```text
primePowerSideTruncated N N h
```

is eventually constant. Its stable value is an algebraic linear functional
`compactPrimePowerLinear`; no prime cancellation or analytic continuation is
needed. This formalizes the local-finiteness claim already made in the essay.

The arithmetic coefficients remain essential. Generic finite sites and
weights produce equally linear functionals. A concrete unit-weight
perturbation differs from the canonical prime-power functional already at
`P = 2`, `R = 1`. Finite support and linearity therefore do not identify the
arithmetic distribution.

## Score Projection

A `CompletedCosineTest` couples a compact log test `h` to an integrable
frequency density `phi` through

```text
integral phi(u) cos(u t) du = (h(t) + h(-t)) / 2.
```

This is upstream Fourier/cosine synthesis, not a reference to the desired
Weil pairing. On every finite prime-power window, integrating the uncentered
Poisson score against `phi` equals the finite prime-power pairing of `h`
exactly.

The repository's energy/score jet uses the centered character
`1 - cos(u t)`. Its projection therefore exposes a forced vacuum term:

```text
integral phi(u) centeredScore(P,R,u) du
  = 2 h(0) primePowerMass(P,R) - primePowerSide(P,R,h).
```

With the jet's negative-score convention, adding
`2 h(0) primePowerMass(P,R)` restores the exact prime-power pairing. The
renormalized diagonal score pairing then eventually stabilizes to
`compactPrimePowerPairing h`. This is the first constructed finite-score
projection from the paired boundary jet.

## Completed Places

The two remaining classical place shapes are analytically different:

- the Gamma term is a real-critical-boundary multiplier generated by
  `logDer Gamma_R(1/2 + iu)`;
- the factor `s(s-1)` is an off-boundary operation, represented on compact
  log tests by the exponential-moment kernel
  `exp(t/2) + exp(-t/2)`.

Both pairings are now concrete. The canonical completed functional fixes the
convention `pole + 2 * Gamma - prime powers`. Its equality with the zero-side
completed Weil distribution is stated precisely but not yet proved.

## The Domain Decision

The repository now uses the essay's `C_c^infinity(R)` core for
`WeilPositivity`, while retaining `C_c(R)` as the larger finite-place
envelope. This distinction matters:

- compact continuity is enough for local finiteness of the prime-power side;
- a cosine density for a self-convolution is available through `L2` Fourier
  theory, but arbitrary continuous compact support need not provide the
  logarithmic frequency decay required by the Gamma multiplier;
- smooth compact support produces a Schwartz Fourier transform, making the
  finite, Gamma, and pole faces share a natural domain.

Three paths were available:

1. use `C_c^infinity` as the canonical completed core while retaining `C_c`
   as the larger finite-place envelope;
2. keep the current `C_c` criterion and prove a nontrivial extension theorem
   for the Gamma distribution;
3. choose a weighted Sobolev/Wiener domain and prove that it contains the
   required self-convolutions.

Path 1 is selected. It matches the essay, gives the shortest route through
Mathlib's `HasCompactSupport.toSchwartzMap` and Schwartz Fourier equivalence,
and keeps enlargement to rougher tests as a later density or continuity
theorem. The smooth compact core now maps canonically to complex Schwartz
space; its Fourier transform remains Schwartz, its squared modulus is
integrable, and self-convolution remains smooth compact. Fourier inversion,
including the exact `2*pi` change of variables, gives every smooth compact
test a canonical integrable cosine density. For the positive
self-convolution sector, the normalized squared Fourier modulus is separately
proved integrable and nonnegative. Its change-of-variables formula is exact,
and translation covariance plus Plancherel prove its Wiener--Khinchin
identification with self-convolution. Its first absolute frequency moment is
also integrable. A fixed Beta-integral majorant and Cauchy's derivative
estimate prove a measurable at-most-linear bound for the critical Gamma
score. Every smooth compact test therefore constructs a `CompletedGammaTest`
unconditionally, including the positive self-convolution sector.

The three place values are assembled into `completedPlaceFunctional` on the
typed smooth core. The matching entire zero transform, nontrivial-zero index,
and analytic multiplicities are constructed in
`BoundaryCompletedFunctional.lean`. The remaining identity needs global
zero-sum convergence and the completed-zeta contour formula; see
`notes/completed-weil-identity-gate.md`. Positivity remains downstream.
